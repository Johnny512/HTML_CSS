Fender Stratocaster

The Fender Stratocaster is a model of electric guitar designed in 1954 by Leo Fender, Bill Carson, George Fullerton, and Freddie Tavares. The Fender Musical Instruments Corporation has continuously manufactured the Stratocaster from 1954 to the present. It is a double-cutaway guitar, with an extended top horn shape for balance.

Gibson Les Paul

The Gibson Les Paul is a solid body electric guitar that was first sold in 1952. The Les Paul was designed by Ted McCarty in collaboration with popular guitarist Les Paul, whom Gibson enlisted to endorse the new model. It is one of the most iconic and widely recognized electric guitars in the world. The Les Paul has been used by many famous guitarists, including Jimmy Page, Slash, and Eric Clapton. It is known for its rich, warm tone and sustain, making it a favorite among rock and blues musicians. Over the years, the Les Paul has undergone various modifications and has been released in numerous versions, each with its own unique features and specifications.
